US Homebuilder Sentiment Holds at 38

The NAHB homebuilder confidence index remains at 38, well below the break-even mark of 50, reflecting continued caution among builders amid higher material costs, elevated mortgage rates, and uncertain demand.

Bay Area: A Split Personality Market

The Bay Area housing market is showing two very different stories this spring. Homes in San Francisco and San Mateo counties are moving fast — many under contract in fewer than 14 days — while inventory in Santa Clara and Alameda counties has roughly doubled since December, giving buyers more room to negotiate.

Santa Clara County

Home prices have dipped 3.5% year-over-year, a notable shift in one of the Bay Area's most competitive markets. Rising inventory and longer days on market are giving buyers leverage they haven't had in years.

Alameda County (Fremont Focus)

The median home price in the Fremont area sits at $925,000. Inventory has expanded significantly since the start of the year, creating more balanced conditions between buyers and sellers heading into April.
