The Bay Area market is in a "Spring Reset" characterized by a widening gap between available inventory and actual closings. While active listings in Santa Clara and San Mateo have more than doubled since December, closed sales have dipped nearly 40% year-over-year, providing a quiet window of opportunity for buyers to negotiate with less competition.
📍 Latest Real Estate News in the Bay Area: Today's reports highlight a "split personality" market across the region. Santa Clara County is currently a regional outlier, seeing a modest year-over-year price dip despite tightening inventory heading into peak spring. Active listings in the county have surged to 1,186 homes — more than double December's levels — while sales fell 42% year-over-year. In Alameda County, the market remains the most accessible entry point with a median price of $925,000, though Fremont and Oakland are seeing longer median days on market compared to the blistering 8-day pace in San Mateo. Savvy buyers are finding that "move-in ready" single-family homes still command multiple offers, while condos face significantly more headwinds and longer listing periods.
📍 Latest General News in the Bay Area: In Santa Clara County, Assembly Speaker Robert Rivas officially announced California Farmworkers Day as a new state holiday to honor labor history and survivors. Local residents in Milpitas and Los Gatos are advised of tree removal work starting today, March 23, through Friday on College Ave, which may impact local traffic. In Fremont, the new Police Chief, Floyd Mitchell, officially begins his tenure this week following his recent appointment from Oakland. Additionally, Fremont residents planning spring travel are being encouraged to utilize the "Home Check" property surveillance programs offered by local police for added security during the vacation season.
