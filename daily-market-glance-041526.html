Numbers as of 4:00pm ET April 14th
Citadel CEO Ken Griffin warned the world could be staring down a recession if the Strait of Hormuz stays closed for 6-12 months. Oil has already jumped from $70 to $100 since the conflict started, and Griffin sees the crisis accelerating the shift to clean energy as buyers hunt for alternatives outside oil.
Producer-side inflation surprised to the upside — in a good way. The Producer Price Index rose just 0.5% in March, less than half the 1.1% Wall Street expected. Energy still surged 8.5%, but costs across the rest of the economy held nearly flat — a quiet sign that inflation pressure may be more contained than the headlines suggest.
