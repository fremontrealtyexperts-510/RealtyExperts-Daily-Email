HELOCs at 3-Year Lows; Boomers Dominate the Market

Home equity loan rates are sitting near 7% — the lowest level in three years. With first-lien rates still in the low-6s, more owners are tapping equity to renovate rather than refinance or move, which is part of what's holding resale supply down.

NAR data shows Boomers (61–79) made up 42% of buyers and 55% of sellers in 2026. Millennials are still the largest owner cohort — they're just sitting on equity and not selling.

Tesla AI5 Chip Lifts NASDAQ; Amazon Buys Globalstar

NASDAQ led again as Tesla jumped 7.6% after unveiling its AI5 chip — Musk says it delivers 8–10x the muscle of the prior AI4 and rivals Nvidia's H100. TSMC and Samsung are slated to manufacture, with production targeted for 2027.

Powell's Replacement on Deck; Tariff Refunds Start April 20

Kevin Warsh — Trump's pick to replace Jerome Powell — heads to the Senate Banking Committee soon. Powell's term ends May 15, and Powell himself reiterated the Fed remains "well positioned to wait" on rate moves, with Cleveland Fed President Hammack echoing the same.

Tariff refund checks start rolling out April 20. After the Supreme Court struck down the Trump tariff plan in February, at least 56,000 of the 330,000 affected businesses — including Costco, Kohl's, FedEx, and Nintendo — are first in line for reimbursement.

Goldman + BlackRock Build BTC ETFs; Pakistan Lifts Crypto Ban

Goldman Sachs and BlackRock are both building new Bitcoin ETFs, with Goldman expected to launch its first soon. If institutional money flows in, expect lower volatility across BTC and the broader crypto basket.

Pakistan ended its 8-year ban on banks working with crypto firms. Licensed crypto companies can now open bank accounts and process payments — a major opening in one of the world's largest retail crypto markets, where millions had been transacting peer-to-peer.
