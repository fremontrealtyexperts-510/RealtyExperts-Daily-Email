Office fire sale: A Chicago tower that traded for $68M in 2016 just sold for $4.2M. A 94% haircut, not a typo. Lowe's (LOW +3.84%) put $250M behind a bet that AI can't climb a ladder. A decade-long plan to recruit and train 250,000 plumbers, carpenters, and electricians. The backdrop: construction firms need 349,000 trade workers by year-end, 92% can't find help, and 47% of skilled tradespeople already out-earn the average college grad. Real wages for real work.
March CPI came in hot at +0.9% monthly. The biggest jump in a while. But gas did almost all the damage at +21.2% on Iran fallout. Strip food and energy and core rose just +0.2%, below forecasts. The sticker shock is narrow (the pump), not broad-based. Which is why the Fed probably keeps its cool.
WTI crude snapped back above $87 after Iran vowed to retaliate for a seized tanker. The Strait of Hormuz story had five plot twists in four days: open Thursday, closed Friday, back open Saturday, US seizure Sunday. Volatility at the pump is the one thing you can count on.
