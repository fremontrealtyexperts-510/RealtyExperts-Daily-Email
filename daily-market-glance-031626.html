U.S. homebuilder sentiment edged up to 38 in March, beating expectations, though sentiment remains below the 50 break-even point for the 23rd straight month. PulteGroup also announced the nomination of Benjamin Schall, CEO of AvalonBay Communities, to its Board of Directors.
The Bay Area market is entering a "steady but not overheating" phase. While median prices remain high at roughly $1.25M region-wide, inventory is projected to rise by 10% this year, helping to ease the intense bidding wars of previous years.
The lock-in effect is still the biggest drag on housing supply. Millions of homeowners who refinanced at 3% during the pandemic have no incentive to sell — they'd have to take on a new mortgage at roughly double that rate. Most economists think rates would need to fall closer to 5% before that logjam meaningfully breaks.
At 6%, buying a home is more affordable than it was a year ago. But the window that was quietly opening over the past few months just got a little narrower.
