Home inspections are back. During the pandemic, buyers waived inspections to win bids. That trend is fading fast. Buyers are asking for inspections again, deals are taking longer, home warranties are making a comeback, and thermal imaging is becoming standard. For our listing agents, this is the playbook shift: pre-listing inspection isn't optional anymore. If your seller skips it, a buyer's inspector will find something in week 3 and you'll renegotiate from a weaker seat. Get ahead of it.
For listing agents: Rates hit a four-week low. Buyers who've been watching from the sidelines have a reason to come off the fence. Price at the last comp, pre-inspect the home, and push for offers in the first 10 days. The window for "rates might drop more" is still open but it only takes one Hormuz headline to slam it shut.

Gulf countries are running out of US dollars thanks to Hormuz closures. A closed Strait means fewer oil sales and fewer dollars recycling back. Some Gulf states are asking Treasury for emergency dollar supply; others are hinting they may turn to the Chinese yuan if dollars run dry. Watch this quietly. It's a crack in the petrodollar.
San Francisco just became the first U.S. city ever to hit $8/gallon diesel. Three culprits: the Iran war (global oil up), California's clean-fuel blend (tight supply), and the highest fuel taxes in the country. Diesel moves trucks and trains, so $8 fuel means higher grocery and restaurant prices are coming.
Oil spiked near $100 on Brent after Iran seized two container ships in the Strait of Hormuz Wednesday. This was hours after Trump extended the ceasefire indefinitely. The White House says seizing non-U.S. ships doesn't violate the ceasefire. Technically fine, narratively not great.
