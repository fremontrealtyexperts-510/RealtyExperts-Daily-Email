Canada's Housing Market Chills; Build-to-Rent Retreats 19%

Our neighbors to the north are forecast to grow just 1% in 2026 home sales, with median new-home prices projected near $688,955. Higher oil-driven inflation triggered rate-hike chatter right at peak buying season — sound familiar?

Single-family build-to-rent starts fell 19% year-over-year — from 84,000 units in 2024 to 68,000 in 2025. Financing costs climbed, rents rose just 1.2%, and the math stopped penciling for investors.

Record Highs; Live Nation Loses Antitrust Case; Robinhood Pops

S&P 500 and NASDAQ both printed fresh record highs this week as traders priced in an earlier end to the Iran conflict. Records make nice headlines — but they don't change how a pullback feels, so stay disciplined.

A federal jury ruled Live Nation (LYV) ran an illegal ticket monopoly, siding with 36 states and D.C. The remedy could force a sell-off of venues and promotion arms — good for competition, bad for a stock built on control.

The SEC scrapped a 30-year-old rule requiring retail day traders to hold $25,000 minimum balances. Robinhood (HOOD) popped 10% on the news. More access does not mean better returns — worth a reminder for clients feeling bold.

WTI Drops 11% on Hormuz News; Labor Stuck in Low-Hire Mode

WTI Crude took an 11% face-plant today after Iran's foreign minister said the Strait of Hormuz will stay open. Supply fears evaporated and the premium baked into oil since the war began came right back out. Gasoline prices should follow in the days ahead — a quiet tailwind for household budgets.

Jobless claims dipped again last week — the labor market is stuck in a low hire, low fire holding pattern. Snapchat is cutting 1,000 jobs (16% of staff), reminding us that low fire doesn't mean zero fire.

The U.S. Treasury put two Chinese banks on notice for allegedly helping Iran move money — sanctions could follow. Iran has reportedly been asking oil tankers to pay in crypto just to keep things moving.

Tether Stacks More BTC; South Korea Pilots Blockchain Deposits

Tether quietly added another $70 million in Bitcoin, lifting reserves to 97,141 BTC (around $7.16 billion). That makes the stablecoin issuer the #2 corporate Bitcoin holder on the planet — second only to MicroStrategy's 780,897-BTC war chest.

South Korea is piloting bank-issued blockchain deposits across nine major banks later this year. Real money becomes programmable digital currency — spending rules and fraud prevention baked directly into the ledger. Watch the rest of Asia follow.
