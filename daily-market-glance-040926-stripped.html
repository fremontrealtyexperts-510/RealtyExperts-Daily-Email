Gold posted its best single day since November, blasting past $4,800 — and it did it while stocks were rallying. That’s unusual. The read? Investors aren’t fully convinced the ceasefire will hold, so they’re grabbing gold as a hedge.
The Fed’s March meeting notes confirmed at least one rate cut is still on the table this year. The ceasefire pushed December cut odds to 43% on CME, with a Fed study suggesting inflation could drop quickly if Middle East oil starts flowing again.
But don’t expect a quick recovery at the pump. Even with the Strait of Hormuz reopening, experts say restarting wells could take 6 to 9 months, and roughly 1,000 ships remain stuck waiting to pass through. The 11 million barrels per day that went offline won’t come back overnight.
President Trump added another wrinkle — any country supplying weapons to Iran will now face 50% tariffs on all goods sold to the U.S. No names were named, but Russia and China are squarely in the crosshairs.
