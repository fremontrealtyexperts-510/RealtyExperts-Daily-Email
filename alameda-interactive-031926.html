Bay Area: Selective Spring Acceleration

The Bay Area market is showing "selective spring acceleration." While inventory is expanding, certain submarkets like Santa Clara are seeing a rare 3.5% year-over-year price dip, offering a strategic window for buyers compared to the double-digit growth in other counties.

Santa Clara County is currently the Bay Area's most significant market outlier, with median home prices experiencing a 3.5% year-over-year decline. This cooling contrasts sharply with San Francisco, where overbidding remains rampant at an average of 16.5% above list price.
