Bay Area "Spring Reset"

The Bay Area market is in a "Spring Reset" characterized by a widening gap between available inventory and actual closings. While active listings in Santa Clara and San Mateo have more than doubled since December, closed sales have dipped nearly 40% year-over-year, providing a quiet window of opportunity for buyers to negotiate with less competition.

Santa Clara County

Currently a regional outlier, seeing a modest year-over-year price dip despite tightening inventory heading into peak spring. Active listings have surged to 1,186 homes — more than double December's levels — while sales fell 42% year-over-year.

Alameda County (Fremont Focus)

The market remains the most accessible entry point with a median price of $925,000, though Fremont and Oakland are seeing longer median days on market compared to the blistering 8-day pace in San Mateo. "Move-in ready" single-family homes still command multiple offers, while condos face significantly more headwinds.
