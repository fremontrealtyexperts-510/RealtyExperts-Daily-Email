National Picture: Million-Dollar Markets and Falling Applications

Mortgage applications fell for the third straight week, even with rates drifting down from the 6.47% high in late March. Some analysts think if the Iran ceasefire holds, rates could test 6% by June. Meanwhile, 13 metro areas now have a median price above $1 million — five of them in California. But even at the top, prices are softening: the top 10% of U.S. home prices fell 2.9% year-over-year.

Fremont Local News

Rabies Warning in Glenmoor: Health officials and the Fremont Police Department have confirmed that a dead bat found in the Glenmoor neighborhood recently tested positive for the rabies virus. Residents are advised to be cautious, avoid handling wild animals, and ensure their pets' vaccinations are up to date.

New Police Chief: Floyd Mitchell has officially taken the helm as Fremont's new Police Chief, bringing his leadership experience from the Oakland Police Department.

Charter City Transition: Fremont is actively exploring a transition from a general law city to a Charter City. The newly appointed Charter Advisory Committee held its latest public meeting on April 6 to discuss potential changes to local governance.

Gold Surges While Stocks Rally — Investors Hedge the Ceasefire

Gold posted its best single day since November, blasting past $4,800 even as stocks rallied. The read? Investors aren't fully convinced the ceasefire will hold, so they're grabbing gold as a hedge.

The Fed's March meeting notes confirmed at least one rate cut is still on the table this year. The ceasefire pushed December cut odds to 43% on CME, with a Fed study suggesting inflation could drop fast if Middle East oil starts flowing again.

But don't expect a quick recovery at the pump. Experts say restarting wells could take 6 to 9 months, with roughly 1,000 ships still stuck near the Strait of Hormuz. The 11 million barrels per day that went offline won't come back overnight. President Trump warned that any country supplying weapons to Iran will face 50% tariffs — a thinly veiled nod to Russia and China.

Morgan Stanley Enters Crypto; Dip Buyers Tighten Supply

Morgan Stanley just launched a spot Bitcoin ETF — the first from a major U.S. investment bank. The kicker? Fees are nearly half what BlackRock charges on its $55 billion Bitcoin fund.

Meanwhile, someone's been buying the war dip. Around 850,000 Bitcoin — roughly 4% of total supply — have changed hands since January. That's tightened available supply significantly, and if demand picks up, the math favors anyone who loaded up.
