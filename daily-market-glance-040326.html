The 30-year fixed has climbed five straight weeks to its highest level since September — and the gap between sellers and buyers just hit a national record.
The seller-buyer gap just hit a record — 630,000 more people looking to sell than looking to buy. Homes are taking 53 days to sell (up from 48 last year), and the average monthly mortgage payment in the U.S. is now $2,742. Rising rates and global instability are keeping buyers on the sidelines.
Total inventory across our 16-city tracker slipped another 25 units to 2,522 — the fourth straight week of decline. Coming-soon held steady at 250, but new listings dropped sharply to 156 (from 207 last week).
📍 Milpitas: The hottest market got even hotter. Total inventory dropped 10 to 113 — the steepest decline of any city this week. Townhome pending (33) exceeds active (23) by 10. Coming-soon steady at 33. Buyers are snapping up homes before they even hit MLS.
📍 Fremont: Down 8 to 294 total. Coming-soon ticked up to 59 (from 50 last week). Townhome actives dropped from 50 to 47, and only 19 new listings. The spring pipeline is building but not releasing.
📍 Union City: Up 5 from last week to 89. Still one of the tightest markets, but inventory is slowly building with 6 new listings.
📍 Newark: Crossed the 100 mark — up 9 from last week to 101. Steady growth continues.
📍 Oakland: Biggest raw decline — down 20 to 736. Buyers absorbing faster than sellers can list.
📍 Dublin: Eased back slightly from 151 to 148, after last week's big jump.
On the national front, the record seller-buyer gap is a signal worth watching. More people want out than want in — and if mortgage rates stay elevated through spring, that gap could widen further.
