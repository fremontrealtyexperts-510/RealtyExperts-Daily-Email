Total inventory across our 15-city tracker held at 2,560 — 246 coming soon and 211 new listings as the spring market stays in motion. Rates ticked up just one basis point, with both 30 and 15-year fixed barely budging from yesterday.

Tax Day Is Here — Refunds Up 11%

The IRS reports that average refunds are running about 11% higher this year. That extra cash often becomes a down payment, an emergency fund, or a paydown on debt that helps qualify for a mortgage. Worth a conversation with any client who's been on the fence about buying.

Big Banks Beat the Tape; Nvidia Kills PC Rumor

Markets pushed higher across the board, with NASDAQ leading at nearly 2%. Big bank earnings stole the show — JPMorgan posted its best quarter ever with over $50 billion in revenue, Citi beat on banking fees, but Wells Fargo missed as its lending-heavy revenue mix weighed on results.

Dell and HP briefly surged on a rumor that Nvidia was eyeing a major PC company acquisition. Nvidia killed the story fast — calling it "completely false" — but the chip giant's own stock has now climbed over 18% in the last two weeks.

Griffin Warns of Recession; PPI Surprises to the Upside

Citadel CEO Ken Griffin warned the world could be staring down a recession if the Strait of Hormuz stays closed for 6-12 months. Oil has already jumped from $70 to $100 since the conflict started, and Griffin sees the crisis accelerating the shift to clean energy as buyers hunt for alternatives.

Producer-side inflation surprised to the upside — in a good way. The Producer Price Index rose just 0.5% in March, less than half the 1.1% Wall Street expected. Energy still surged 8.5%, but costs across the rest of the economy held nearly flat — a quiet sign that inflation pressure may be more contained than the headlines suggest.

Crypto ETFs Roar Back; Kraken Files for IPO

Crypto ETFs had their best week since January, pulling in $1.1 billion in combined inflows. Bitcoin led with $871M, while Ethereum brought in $196.5M — finally snapping three straight weeks of outflows. Analysts credit the Iran ceasefire and softer inflation data for the renewed appetite.

Crypto exchange Kraken filed for an IPO yesterday, following Coinbase and Gemini onto public markets. Some estimates peg Kraken's valuation at $13 billion — and Deutsche Borse's recent $200 million stake (1.5%) suggests institutional money is already lining up.
